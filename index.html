"LONGCLAW" High Powered Rocket

Longclaw is a model rocket made out of cardboard body tube and 3D printed components. It uses the Longclaw PCB I designed for data logging and parachutte deployment. It files on an F10-0 Estes rocket motor and flew unsuccessfully twice. First flight there was a timing issue in my code and the second time, after a complete rebuild, it failed because of an issue in my PCB wiring. Lots was learned.

"LONGCLAW PCB" Flight Computer

This board family is the one that flew each time on the Longclaw rocket. Much of the board development was done on breadboards for proof of concept. Then once validated, I transfered the design to a PCB designed in Altium Designer to gain experience in that software. The PCB mainly functions as a breakout board for orginization, with an IMU, MPU, SD card slot, psuh button, and MOSFET on an Arduino Nano.

"AUTUMN" High Powered Rocket

This is my successful level 1 certification rocket. It flew once on an Aerotech H135 motor down in Pueblo Colorado. This rocket however, is a kit assembled by me. Reached a speed around mach 0.75 and a heigh of about 1 kilometer.
